# Indian Legal Reference — Deepfake / Digital Forgery

Accurate as of the BNS 2023 (effective 1 July 2024), which replaced the IPC.
Sources should be re-verified against cybercrime.gov.in / a legal professional
before any real use — this is a triage aid, not legal advice.

---
id: bns_336_forgery
applies_to: [image, video]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: BNS Section 336 covers forgery of electronic records. Section 336(3) applies to forgery committed with intent to cheat (relevant to deepfake voice/video scams and impersonation-based fraud), carrying up to 7 years imprisonment. Section 336(4) covers forgery intended to harm someone's reputation, carrying up to 3 years.
---
id: bns_340_using_forged
applies_to: [image, video, audio]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: BNS Section 340 extends liability to anyone who knowingly uses a forged electronic record as if it were genuine — relevant if the flagged media is being circulated or presented as authentic by someone other than its creator.
---
id: bns_318_cheating_personation
applies_to: [audio, video]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: BNS Section 318 covers cheating by personation, directly relevant to deepfake-enabled financial fraud (e.g., voice-cloned "urgent transfer" scams impersonating executives or family members).
---
id: bns_353_public_mischief
applies_to: [image, video]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: BNS Section 353 penalizes making or circulating false statements, rumours, or reports likely to cause public mischief or fear — relevant if the flagged media is being used to spread misinformation.
---
id: bns_111_organised_crime
applies_to: [image, video, audio]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: BNS Section 111 (organised crime) can apply where deepfake content is part of a coordinated operation — e.g., large-scale scam call centers or coordinated disinformation campaigns, not a single isolated incident.
---
id: it_act_66c_identity_theft
applies_to: [image, video]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: IT Act Section 66C covers identity theft, applicable when someone's identifying features (face, biometric-like data) are used without authorization to create the flagged content.
---
id: it_act_66d_impersonation
applies_to: [audio, video]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: IT Act Section 66D covers cheating by personation using a computer resource — the primary provision for voice/video impersonation scams.
---
id: it_act_67_67a_obscene
applies_to: [image, video]
condition: nsfw_or_sensitive
text: IT Act Sections 67 and 67A address publishing or transmitting obscene or sexually explicit material electronically, with enhanced penalties under 67A — relevant only if the flagged content is of that nature and non-consensual.
---
id: dpdp_act_consent
applies_to: [image, video, audio]
condition: high_confidence_fake
text: The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 requires lawful, consent-based processing of personal data. Generating a deepfake from someone's photos/voice without consent can itself be a separate violation under this Act, independent of the forgery charge.
---
id: reporting_portal
applies_to: [image, video, audio]
condition: any
text: Complaints can be filed directly at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) in addition to, or before, an in-person police report — useful to note in any triage report as a next step for the complainant.
---
