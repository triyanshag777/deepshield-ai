# Forensic Indicator Knowledge Base

Each entry: a named indicator, the signal it maps to, and a plain-language
explanation. The RAG explainer retrieves the most relevant entries for the
signals that fired on a given input and cites them in the report.

---
id: ela_high
signal: ela
text: High Error Level Analysis residual suggests inconsistent compression history across regions of the image. Real camera photos compress fairly uniformly; images composited or generated by AI models often show sharp discontinuities in compression noise where synthetic regions meet real ones or where an entire region was generated at once.
---
id: ela_low
signal: ela
text: Low, uniform Error Level Analysis residual across the whole frame can indicate the entire image was generated in one pass by a diffusion or GAN model rather than captured by a camera sensor, since it lacks the localized compression variance typical of edited real photos.
---
id: fft_high
signal: fft_noise
text: Elevated high-frequency energy relative to low-frequency energy in the Fourier spectrum is a known fingerprint of GAN upsampling layers (transposed convolutions), which introduce periodic grid-like artifacts invisible to the eye but detectable in frequency space.
---
id: fft_diffusion
signal: fft_noise
text: Diffusion-based generators (like Stable Diffusion or Midjourney) tend to produce unnaturally smooth high-frequency spectra compared to real camera sensor noise, which has characteristic Poisson-distributed grain from the sensor readout process.
---
id: face_symmetry_high
signal: face_symmetry
text: Abnormally high facial asymmetry, especially in accessories (earrings, glasses) or hairlines, is a common GAN face-generation artifact, since generative models often struggle to keep paired features (like two earrings) mutually consistent.
---
id: face_symmetry_low
signal: face_symmetry
text: Unnaturally perfect left-right facial symmetry is statistically rare in real human faces and can indicate a synthetic or heavily processed face, since generative models sometimes over-regularize facial structure.
---
id: model_ensemble_agree
signal: model_ensemble
text: Multiple independently-trained deepfake classifiers (different architectures and training data) agreeing on a fake verdict substantially reduces the chance of a false positive from any single model's blind spot or dataset bias.
---
id: model_ensemble_disagree
signal: model_ensemble
text: Disagreement between classifier architectures suggests the image sits near a decision boundary; forensic signals (ELA, FFT, symmetry) should be weighted more heavily than the model verdict alone in this case.
---
id: temporal_flicker
signal: temporal
text: Frame-to-frame inconsistency in fake-probability across a video is a signature of face-swap or lip-sync deepfakes, which are typically generated frame-by-frame or with imperfect temporal smoothing, unlike real video which has continuous, gradually-changing pixel statistics.
---
id: audio_synthetic
signal: audio
text: Synthetic voice detectors trained on Wav2Vec2 embeddings pick up on unnaturally regular pitch contours, timing precision, and energy patterns in near-silence regions that differ from the natural variability of human speech production.
